COMP 1950 Website Redesign

Created by Team Awesome

Introduction

Team Awesome is passionate about creative and contemporary website design and development. To better meet the needs of students, we've updated the COMP 1950 course website to demonstrate a variety of web development techniques that directly relate to the course content, including the use of HTML5, responsive CSS3, javascript, jquery and dynamic content. In addition, the overall site structure, navigation, fonts and colour scheme have been optimized to enhance student learning.

Team Awesome members are Danielle Roussey and Carole Hall. We collaborated throughout the project but responsibilities were delegated as follows:

  • Team Representative: Danielle Roussy
  • Strategy and Planning: Danielle and Carole
  • Meta Site (content, design, development): Carole Hall
  • Proto-site (content, design, development): Danielle Roussy

Proto-site

Mock ups of four key pages are included in the proto-site.

Home Page

The new home page dashboard and aesthetic design will engage students and provide easy access to all of the course components.

Lectures

Lecture notes are well-organized with agenda, curriculum, exercises, and assignments sections. Each can be directly accessed through the scrolling navigation.

Exercises

Students can now conveniently download all exercises from one page, rather than scanning through all of the lesson pages on the site.

On-Line Quizzes

The new on-line quizzes will be easy to customize and administer. Results can also be tracked electronically to facilitate the evaluation of students' learning progress.

Awesome Features

User-Friendly

Students will have easy access to lecture notes, exercises, on-line quizzes, assignments, and other resources.

Searchable Content

In addition to well-structured navigation panels, the new search field will enable students to find content throughout the new website.

Aesthetic Design

The new site has been designed with an optimal colour palette and font selection to enhance student learning.

Learning Objectives

Now the site demonstrates many of the course learning objectives, including standards compliant web development, HTML5, CSS3, and methodology-driven design.

Enable/disable Options

Instructors can enable/disable course components (lessons, exercises, quizzes) so that they are available to students at the appropriate time.

Browser & Device Compatability

Works on Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Opera, Safari and Internet Explorer, using all operating systems. And multiple stylesheets enhance viewing with desktops, laptops, and even projectors.
