User-Friendly Navigation

The new website architecture is logically structured to ensure that students have easy access to lecture notes, exercises, on-line quizzes, assignments, and other resources. With the clear and consistent navigation menu on all pages, students will quickly become familiar with the site and know where to find everything they're looking for. The URL structure also demonstrates the logical organization of the site.

In addition, the in-class exercises, that were previously scattered throughout the individual lecture pages, can now be downloaded from one location. Likewise, all assignments are now on their own pages for convenient access.

Searchable Content

In addition to the better architecture and navigation menus, there is a new search field to help students find what they are looking for. This will further improve student access to learning materials and resources.

On a content-heavy website a search box is an essential design element for users, but there are many other benefits as well. The search box may provide a valuable analytics tool to track which content students are most commonly referencing. This information may possibly serve as a teaching aide to identify areas of the content that may require further discussion. It can also highlight areas of the site that may benefit from further restructuring/redesign. And, it can help with search engine optimization.

Aesthetic Design

Using the latest advances in colour theory, the new aesthetic design is visually appealing and improves student engagement.

The psychology of colour plays an imporant role in swaying attitudes and emotions and, therefore, effective learning. Keeping this in mind, the new COMP 1950 website was developed with an ideal colour palette and font selection to appeal to the wide demographic of students in the course and enhance student learning.

Demonstrated Learning Objectives

The new COMP 1950 website demonstrates many of the learning objectives of the course:

  • standards compliant web development
  • HTML5
  • response CSS3
  • javascript frameworks
  • dynamic content
  • search engine optimization
  • methodology-driven design

Now students not only learn about these topics, but can also see them in use.

Enable/Disable Option

The availability of lecture notes, exercises, quizzes, and assignments can be controlled by the instructor. A simple enable/disable toggle enables the instructor to make lessons, exercises, and quizzes available at the appropriate time.

Browser & Device Compatibility

The new COMP 1950 site works flawlessly with all search engines, including Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Opera, Safari and even Internet Explorer. It is also compatible with all operating systems (Windows, Mac, Linux).

In addition, multiple stylesheets will enhance viewing on different media such as desktops, laptops, and even projectors. For students that prefer hard copies of the course content, a printer optimized stylesheet is also provided.

On-line Quizzes

The new on-line quiz feature will improve the evaluation of students' learning progress. Quizzes are easily customizable by simply selecting the applicable questions from the Question Library. A variety of question types in both objective and subjective formats are available.

An additional add-on feature, would enable the tracking and reporting of results. Once marked, you can view quiz statistics such as grade distribution, grade average, question statistics, and user statistics. A grade distribution chart with class average may also be made availabe to students on the Quizzes main page so that they can gauge their progress.
